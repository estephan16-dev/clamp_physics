Pure dead time precedes all observable kinematics. RT is the afferent-cortical-efferent loop; EMD is the electromechanical delay of the series elastic component. During EMD, force rises at rate dF/dt while displacement remains null. Pre-load (light hands, weight on the balls of the feet) shortens the effective EMD by pre-tensioning the SEC.

§3 ROTATIONAL INERTIA, I∥ (ROLL ABOUT SHAFT AXIS)

Iforearm (×2 arms)·
I∥ = Icrosse,∥ + 2Iforearm·

The clamp is a roll about the shaft's own long axis, through the hands. Mass elements separated along that axis contribute ~0 to I — so hand spacing (grip width) does NOT change I∥. The roll inertia is dominated by the forearms/hands that co-rotate with the shaft (mFA ≈ 1.7 kg, rFA ≈ 0.042 m each), not by the crosse itself (Icrosse,∥ ≈ 5×10⁻⁴ kg·m², a slender rod about its own axis). Grip width still matters — for the §4 drive lever.

METHODS NOTE. Rigid multi-body idealization; the crosse is a slender homogeneous rod plus point head mass, actuated at constant net torque over the clamp interval, hence the closed-form θ = ½αt′². Hertzian contact assumes smooth frictionless normal indentation of an elastic sphere on an elastic half-space; the turf interface uses an amontons-coulomb friction law with rate-independent coefficients. Neuromechanical latency is decomposed as serial RT plus EMD with first-order force rise. All displayed quantities are computed analytically from the parameter state at the slider time; the rendered geometry interpolates the same trajectory, so the visual and numerical layers cannot disagree.
